2020 Reflection

"The challenges of remote learning during COVID-19 highlighted the importance of technology, early intervention, and data-driven support. My most successful practice was the development of the Academic Intelligence System (AIS), which proactively identified and supported at-risk students, ensuring no one was left behind. I also implemented online polls and quizzes to check understanding in real time, and created a COVID impact sharing activity where students could express their challenges and support each other. These approaches fostered resilience, inclusivity, and timely intervention, exceeding my expectations for student engagement and support.

However, not all activities were as effective. Some students struggled with the transition to online learning, particularly those less comfortable with digital tools or hesitant to participate in online polls. The COVID impact sharing activity, while supportive for many, was difficult for some students who felt uncomfortable sharing personal experiences in a group setting. I learned that while technology and community-building activities can bridge gaps, they can also create new ones if not all students are equally prepared or comfortable.

In the future, I aimed to provide more scaffolding, digital literacy support, and options for private participation to ensure equitable access and engagement for all. The lessons of 2020—resilience, inclusivity, and the power of timely data and empathy—became the basis for continuous improvement in the years to follow."

2021 Reflection

"Building on the foundation of AIS, I created Mohitso—a bespoke Power BI system tailored for our campus, and a plagiarism management system to reinforce academic integrity. I also tested gamification strategies inspired by the Mario Effect, creating a series of online quizzes as ice tasks. The first iteration focused on theory-based questions (e.g., understanding supply and demand shocks) accessible via QR codes in class, with unlimited attempts and instant feedback including relevant theory for incorrect answers. While students only attempted these once or twice, the second iteration used real-life scenarios and JSE-listed companies as case studies. This change transformed the activity into a game-like challenge—students made multiple attempts, and 97% achieved 100% as they were more engaged and interested in the case study format. This approach actively closed knowledge gaps and demonstrated the power of context and gamification in learning.

However, the rollout of the plagiarism system revealed that some students felt targeted or misunderstood the rehabilitative intent. I learned that clear communication and student involvement in policy design are crucial for buy-in and effectiveness. In the future, I aimed to co-create guidelines with students, provide more context for new systems, and continue refining gamified activities to ensure innovations are both effective and positively received. The year’s focus on data-driven engagement, ethical culture, and gamification set the stage for research-driven improvements and more individualized support in 2022."

2022 Reflection

2022 was a milestone year as I was recognized as the Inspired Teaching winner for my work on the Automated Research Title Generator project. This project leveraged a Socratic-inspired, technology-driven approach using Microsoft Forms, Power Automate, and SharePoint to guide students through the process of generating research titles and abstracts. The Socratic questioning embedded in the system encouraged critical thinking and deeper engagement, resulting in more meaningful research topics and improved student ownership of learning.

A key innovation was the abstract creator, which not only supported students in developing high-quality abstracts but also assisted staff with conference submissions—achieving a 100% acceptance rate for all abstracts generated through the system. This dual impact demonstrated the scalability and effectiveness of the approach for both student and staff development.

Students benefited from structured digital materials, iterative self-assessment, and peer discussions, which deepened their understanding of research principles. However, not all activities met expectations. A prisoner's dilemma exercise designed to teach strategic decision-making resulted in students consistently choosing betrayal over cooperation, highlighting a need for clearer guidance and context. I learned that complex simulations require more scaffolding and real-world examples to achieve desired learning outcomes. In the future, I plan to integrate more background on game theory, facilitate discussions on trust and collaboration, and use student feedback to refine activities. The lesson: personalized, timely support and scalable systems are key to fostering both academic excellence and a sense of belonging. These insights informed my approach to scaling impact and supporting national initiatives in 2023.

2023 Reflection

"In 2023, I drew from lessons learned in previous years and built similar systems to support activities. I used AI to transform past paper questions into multiple choice quizzes, where correct answers triggered positive reinforcement and incorrect answers encouraged retrying after explaining the knowledge gap. Unexpectedly, students began intentionally answering incorrectly to access the full notes for each learning objective. However, this led to deeper engagement—students started discussing and researching the companies featured in the quizzes, especially as many had signed up for the trading challenge. They began linking real-life decisions to business theory, showing authentic learning connections.

For Accounting Software, we co-created notes using different AI chatbots. Some students enjoyed the efficiency, but others found the pure theory aspects of SAGE disengaging. Without a tangible link to the importance of the learning objective, interest waned and we returned to active practice on the SAGE system. I then used AI chatbots to help develop complete SAGE activities, which we worked through in stages, ensuring the examples were based on relevant SAGE theory. This approach restored engagement and provided practical context.

Scaling to larger student cohorts presented new challenges and opportunities for innovation. The most successful practice was expanding the Trading Challenge, which increased participation and engagement through mentorship, dedicated platforms, and new resources. However, not all students benefited equally—some felt overwhelmed by the scale or struggled to access support. I learned that as programs grow, maintaining personal connections and clear communication becomes more difficult but even more important. In the future, I aimed to implement tiered mentorship, more accessible resources, and regular feedback loops to ensure all students feel supported. The lesson: sustainable impact requires both scalable systems and strong networks of support. These experiences inspired me to further leverage technology for personalized learning and skills development in 2024."

2024 Reflection

In 2024, I deepened my commitment to research-informed teaching by integrating Ecotana, an AI-powered personal tutor system, into my curriculum. This innovation allowed me to deliver bespoke support to students after each learning unit. Using carefully engineered prompts aligned with specific learning objectives, Ecotana adapted question paths based on individual knowledge gaps. Students received personalized AI-generated notes only after demonstrating sufficient engagement—either scoring above 50% or attempting the quiz more than three times. This approach created a safe, gamified environment for formative assessment, with 4 out of 5 students reporting increased confidence and appreciation for the opportunity to “test what they knew” without fear of judgment. The system’s ability to identify gaps and provide targeted feedback significantly improved student preparedness and engagement.

However, the integration of AI also surfaced unexpected challenges. Some students noticed discrepancies between textbook theory and AI-generated content, which initially caused doubt in the system’s credibility. Rather than suppress these concerns, I leveraged them to foster critical thinking. Students were encouraged to “pick sides” and use any academic resources to argue their position—either in support of the textbook or the AI’s interpretation. This sparked lively debates and deepened theoretical understanding, especially in abstract areas of economics. The result was a 25% increase in the module success rate (MSR), driven by students’ ability to cross-reference multiple sources and engage in peer-led discussions. The activity transformed passive learning into active inquiry, empowering students to construct their own logic and challenge authoritative knowledge.

From this experience, I learned that while AI can personalize and scale support, it must be balanced with strategies that promote community and critical engagement. To ensure equitable access, I introduced baseline evaluations and quizzes tailored to Ecotana’s insights, with unlimited attempts to encourage mastery over memorization. Some students gamed the system to unlock notes, but even this led to meaningful learning outcomes. Moving forward, I plan to refine hybrid support models that combine AI-driven personalization with peer interaction and lecturer guidance. I will also use analytics to identify students at risk of disengagement earlier in the semester. The ongoing challenge remains: to ensure that innovation serves all students—not just the digitally fluent—by fostering inclusive, critical, and connected learning environments.

Conclusion: A Journey of Growth & Impact

Reflecting on Achievements and the Power of Mindful Education

Over the years, this teaching journey has been defined by a commitment to innovation, resilience, and the pursuit of excellence. Each challenge has been an opportunity to learn, adapt, and grow—both as an educator and as a mentor to future leaders. Achievements in academic performance, research, and student engagement are not just milestones, but stepping stones in a story of continuous development.

Mindful education has been at the heart of this journey: fostering critical thinking, encouraging students to question, reflect, and connect theory to the real world. Technology has been leveraged not just for efficiency, but as a tool to advance equity ambitions—ensuring every student, regardless of background, has access to meaningful learning opportunities.

Individual and approachable support has empowered students to overcome obstacles, while a culture of grit and resilience has reframed failure as a vital part of growth. The goal has always been to nurture responsible, socially conscious citizens—equipped with the skills, knowledge, and mindset to thrive in a complex world.

As this portfolio demonstrates, the true measure of success lies not only in results, but in the ability to inspire, support, and transform lives through education.
